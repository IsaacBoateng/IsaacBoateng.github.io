Realty Rise Real estate Agency

Project overview

The product: Realty Rise is an inclusive tool designed to assist individuals from all walks of life in discovering remarkable houses at affordable prices. Whether you're a first-time homebuyer, a growing family, a young professional, or anyone seeking their dream home, Realty Rise is here to simplify your search. Our platform caters to the diverse needs of college students, adults, and individuals from various backgrounds who value finding quality homes within their budget and are eager to explore opportunities for securing affordable housing.

Challenge: Statistics reveal that a significant portion of housing seekers struggle to find great houses at affordable prices in the United States. The strategy team at Realty Rise has identified a lack of general knowledge about affordable housing options and limited understanding about personal impact as crucial barriers for individuals seeking quality homes within their budget.

Solution: Design an app that will empower individuals by providing valuable information and resources to improve their understanding of affordable housing options and help them effectively manage their home search process.

My role: UX designer leading the app and responsive website design from conception to delivery.

Responsibilities: Conducting interviews, paper and digital wireframing, low and high-fidelity prototyping, conducting usability studies, accounting for accessibility, iterating on designs, determining information architecture, and responsive design.

Understanding the user

Target Audience: People who are too busy to Look for the best properties at affordable prices

User research: summary Using Realty Rise's data on affordable housing challenges, I developed interview questions that were utilized during user interviews. The majority of interview participants expressed frustration with the difficulty of finding great houses at affordable prices but admitted to not actively taking steps to address this issue. The research feedback indicated a strong willingness among users to actively engage in their home search process if they had access to an intuitive tool that could provide guidance and simplify the search for affordable housing.

Competitive audit: An audit of a few competitor's products provided direction on gaps and opportunities to address with the Realty Rise Website.

Ideation: I did a quick ideation exercise to come up with ideas for how to address gaps identified in the competitive audit. My focus was specifically on Booking a tour and making sure its a fast process.

Starting the design

Digital wireframes: After ideating and drafting some paper wireframes, I created the initial designs for the Realty Rise app. These designs focused on delivering personalized guidance to users to help manage their house choices.

Low-fidelity prototype: To prepare for usability testing, I created a low-fidelity prototype that connected the user flow of viewing an hottest houses on discounts .

Usability study

Findings: These were the main findings uncovered by the usability study:

Website navigation People want easy access to how to get back to the top of the website.

Contact agents People want there to be a direct contact of each agent.

Book a tour People preferred confirmation to be faster.

Refining the design

Mockups:

Based on the insights from the usability studies, I applied design changes like providing a button at the footer to aid in easy navigation to the top.

Mockups:

Additional design changes included adding videos to the featured Property section to make property viewing easier.

Key mockups:

High-fidelity prototype: The high-fidelity prototype followed the same user flow as the low-fidelity prototype, including design changes made after the usability study.

Accessibility considerations:

Clear labels for interactive elements that can be read by screen readers.

Initial focus of the home screen on personalized recommendations help define the primary task or action for the user.

Responsive Design

Sitemap: With the app designs completed, I started work on designing the responsive website. I used the Realty Rise sitemap to guide the organizational structure of each screen's design to ensure a cohesive and consistent experience across devices.

Responsive designs: The designs for screen size variation included phones, tablet, and desktop. I optimized the designs to fit specific user needs of each device and screen size.

Takeaways:

Impact: Users expressed that the real estate platform made the process of finding and acquiring a new home feel attainable and within their reach. One user remarked in feedback, "The Realty Rise platform brings the dream of homeownership to a personal level, making it accessible and engaging, while fostering a sense of care for one's housing needs and aspirations."

What I learned: Through my real estate exploration, I discovered that despite the magnitude of the challenge at hand, adopting a meticulous approach in each stage of the design process and closely aligning with the unique requirements of users enabled me to generate practical and valuable solutions.
