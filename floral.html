Blooming Delights Flower shop

Project overview

The product: Blooming Delights is a charming flower shop nestled in the heart of a vibrant city. Our mission at Blooming Delights is to offer an exquisite collection of flowers and floral arrangements that bring joy and beauty to every occasion. We take pride in providing a diverse range of affordable options to cater to different tastes and budgets. With a special emphasis on catering to individuals with busy lifestyles, we strive to serve those who seek convenient ways to express their love and appreciation through the language of flowers. Whether you're a busy professional or a devoted family person, Blooming Delights is here to make your floral experience delightful and hassle-free.

Challenge: Hectic professionals and urban travelers often find themselves facing a scarcity of time required to tend to their floral desires.

Solution: Design an app for Blooming Delights that enables users to effortlessly browse and select from our exquisite collection of fresh and vibrant floral arrangements.

My role: UX designer designing an app for Blooming delights flower shop from conception to delivery.

Responsibilities: Conducting interviews, paper and digital wireframing, low and high-fidelity prototyping, conducting usability studies, accounting for accessibility, and iterating on designs.

Understanding the user

Target Audience: For individuals who lead busy lives and seek effortless access to quality flowers, Blooming Delights is here to cater to your floral desires.

Summary: I conducted interviews and created empathy maps to gain a deep understanding of the users I am designing for and their specific needs. Through extensive research, it became clear that a primary user group for Blooming Delights consists of working adults who have limited time to tend to their floral desires. However, as the study progressed, it revealed that time constraints were not the sole factor preventing users from enjoying the beauty of flowers at home. The research uncovered various other challenges faced by our users, including different responsibilities, interests, or difficulties in acquiring flowers or visiting physical flower shops. This valuable insight allows Blooming Delights to go beyond a simple understanding of time limitations and address the diverse needs of our customers by providing solutions that cater to their unique obligations, preferences, and challenges in the world of flowers.

Starting the design

Digital wireframes: As the initial design phase continued, I made sure to base screen designs on feedback and findings from the user research.

Low-fidelity prototype: Using the completed set of digital wireframes, I created a low-fidelity prototype. The primary user flow I connected was browsing through flowers and placing a flower order, so the prototype could be used in a usability study.

Usability study

Findings: I conducted two rounds of usability studies. Findings from the first study helped guide the designs from wireframes to mockups. The second study used a high-fidelity prototype and revealed what aspects of the mockups needed refining.

Round 1 findings:
  1. Users want a contact page
  2. Users want a top button added to the footer
  3. Users want Instant verification
Round 2 findings:
  1. The checkout process has too many unnecessary steps
  2. “Sorting for the best flower” was is tideous

Refining the design

Mockups:

Early designs did not have a map, user complain about having to use an external map which prolonged their experience on the app. A map has been added to the home page as requested.

Mockups:

The usability study revealed frustration with the footer and how users find it hard to get back up. As requested a top button has been added to the footer to aid with getting back up.

Key mockups:

High-fidelity prototype: The final high-fidelity prototype showcased improved user flows, offering a seamless experience for selecting and checking out with our exquisite floral offerings. By refining the interface, we were able to enhance the clarity and efficiency of the process, ensuring that users can effortlessly navigate through the selection of flowers and proceed to checkout with ease. Additionally, the prototype successfully addressed user feedback by streamlining the signup and checkout processes, enabling quicker and more convenient interactions.

Accessibility considerations:

Ensured inclusivity and accessibility for users with visual impairments by implementing alt text descriptions for images, enabling screen readers to provide them with access to our floral offerings. By adding descriptive text to the images, we aimed to provide an inclusive browsing experience, allowing individuals with vision impairments to fully engage with the beauty and details of our flowers.

Used icons to help make navigation easier.

Utilized detailed imagery to enhance the flower selection experience, enabling all users to gain a better understanding and appreciation of our exquisite floral designs.

Takeaways:

Impact: The app creates a sense of personalized care, making users feel that Blooming Delights genuinely prioritizes meeting their unique floral needs.

What I learned: During the design process of the Blooming Delights app, I discovered that the initial concepts for the app are merely the inception of the journey. The incorporation of usability studies and constructive input from peers significantly shaped each subsequent iteration of the app's designs.

One quote from peer feedback: “The app rendered the process of choosing my floral arrangements and coordinating complementary blooms so effortless and enjoyable. I would unquestionably utilize this app as my primary resource for a delightful, prompt, and even healthful floral experience.”
