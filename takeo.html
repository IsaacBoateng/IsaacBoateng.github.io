Isaac's Takeout Restaurant

Project overview

The product: Isaac's Takeouts is a local food producer situated in the outskirts of a bustling metropolis. The primary goal of Isaac's Takeouts is to provide nutritious and distinctive food items as well as complementary dishes. They ensure a diverse range of affordable options to cater to various budget preferences. With a focus on busy individuals such as commuters and workers, Isaac's Takeouts aims to serve those who face time constraints or find it challenging to prepare a satisfying dinner for their families

Challenge: Busy workers and commuters are often faced with a shortage of time needed to prepare a meal.

Solution: Design an app for Isaac's Takeouts that allows users to easily order and pick up fresh, healthy dishes.

My role: UX designer designing an app for Isaacs's Takout Restaurant from conception to delivery.

Responsibilities: Conducting interviews, paper and digital wireframing, low and high-fidelity prototyping, conducting usability studies, accounting for accessibility, and iterating on designs.

Understanding the user

Target Audience: People who are too busy to cook and would love to have easy access to healthy foods.

Summary: I conducted interviews and created empathy maps to understand the users I'm designing for and their needs. A primary user group identified through research was working adults who don't have time to cook meals. The user group confirmed the initial assumptions regarding Isaac's Takeouts' customers, but further research uncovered that time constraints were not the sole factor preventing users from cooking at home. The study revealed additional user challenges, including various obligations, interests, or difficulties in obtaining groceries for cooking or visiting restaurants in person.

Starting the design

Digital wireframes: As the initial design phase continued, I made sure to base screen designs on feedback and findings from the user research.

Low-fidelity prototype: Using the completed set of digital wireframes, I created a low-fidelity prototype. The primary user flow I connected was creating a profile and ordering food, so the prototype could be used in a usability study.

Usability study

Findings: I conducted two rounds of usability studies. Findings from the first study helped guide the designs from wireframes to mockups. The second study used a high-fidelity prototype and revealed what aspects of the mockups needed refining.

Round 1 findings:
  1. Users want to sign up quickly
  2. Users want videos of food to aid in choosing food
  3. Users want Instant verification
Round 2 findings:
  1. The checkout process has too many unnecessary steps
  2. “Foods to buy together” functionality is confusing

Refining the design

Mockups:

Early designs had a lot of required information, but after the usability studies, It was limited to only the information needed at the profile creation stage.

Mockups:

The usability study revealed frustration with the food choice and which foods to purchase together. As requested a video was added to aid food selection.

Key mockups:

High-fidelity prototype: The final high-fidelity prototype presented cleaner user flows for selecting food and checkout. It also met user needs for quicker signup and checkout.

Accessibility considerations:

Provided access to users who are vision impaired through adding alt text to images for screen readers.

Used icons to help make navigation easier.

Used detailed Imagery and videos for food selection to help all users better understand the designs.

Takeaways:

Impact: The app makes users feel like Isaac's Takeouts really thinks about how to meet their needs.

What I learned: While designing the Isaac's takeout app, I learned that the first ideas for the app are only the beginning of the process. Usability studies and peer feedback influenced each iteration of the app's designs.

One quote from peer feedback: “The app made it so easy and fun to select my food and add dishes that goes together. I would definitely use this app as a go-to for a delicious, fast, and even healthy meal.”
